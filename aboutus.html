About The Bride

Shanna Hively is the daughter of Greg and Jan Hively. She grew up in Fremont Nebraska and is an only child. Shanna knew from an early age that she loved writing and teaching, and went to the University of Nebraska-Lincoln where she majored in English Literature with an emphasis in creative writing. During her time in college, she was a member of the Phi Mu sorority and never missed a Husker football game. After college, Shanna moved down to Kansas City, Missouri to be near Dan. She worked as an Early Head Start Teacher for a year before becoming a graduate student at the University of Central-Missouri where she is obtaining her Masters of Teaching. Some of the things Shanna enjoys in her free time are baking, writing, biking and playing the piano. She is very excited to marry Dan and can’t wait for the wedding.

About The Groom

Dan Wiechert is the son of Dawn and Steve Wiechert. Dan grew up in Woodridge, Illinois – a suburb of Chicago and has a younger sister named Natalie. Dan has always been interested in technology and decided to major in computer science and mathematics at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. During Dan’s time in college he was busy with various internships and always making time for friends. Dan also really enjoyed Husker football and basketball games. After college, Dan took an offer for a job as a software engineer in Kansas City, Missouri. Some of the things that Dan likes to do in his free time are video games, working out, developing new software and watching the Chicago Bears. Dan is very excited for the wedding and to go to the Bahamas afterwards.

Our Story

One night during her freshman year in college, Shanna was studying with a friend, Connie, for a psychology exam she had later that week. They were studying in a room in Harper Hall where they both lived in the dorm rooms. Connie asked Shanna if she wanted to go hang out with these guys she met earlier that week, and like any college student, hanging out with guys sounded a lot more fun than studying! Connie and Shanna then went to floor eight where she met Dan, Greg and Travis. They hung out for a while and soon Dan asked Connie if Shanna was dating anyone. Dan found out that Shanna was single and soon asked for her number. Dan and Shanna would meet up for dinners at Harper Hall and would hang out whenever they had some free time. By the end of November of their freshman year, they were dating – and the rest is history!

The Proposal

It was a Monday, and Dan and Shanna were visiting family in Chicago for his cousin Julie’s wedding that happened over the weekend. It was early in the morning when Dan’s sister Natalie came to ask Shanna if she would go to a park with her and her dog Roxie. Natalie had always said that Roxie needed daily walks and daily runs, so it was no surprise when Natalie asked this to Shanna. Shanna accepted, and then Natalie, Dan and her all hopped into Natalie’s car and drove to the park to let Roxie run.

When they all arrived at the park, Shanna could tell that something was about to happen because Natalie started to say, “Oh look at these pretty flowers,” she was staying behind taking pictures, which Shanna thought was kind of weird. Dan then took Shanna to a peaceful spot and got down on one knee. Shanna doesn’t remember a single thing Dan said, but she knew that he was popping the question and of course, she said yes!

After the proposal, it was filled with lots of family time and congratulations. There were a lot of visitors and a small gathering at the Wiecherts’ house. Then there was something totally unexpected that happened. For dinner, the family all went to one of the couple’s favorite restaurants in Dan’s hometown – Tai San – where the owner found out that they were all celebrating the couple’s engagement. The owner then went to the glass case and picked out a gorgeous vase from China to give to the couple. It was definitely a great ending to the perfect day.
